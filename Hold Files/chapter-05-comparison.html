11. World = Perceptual Framework ("Natural Grand Division")

In the Course's original dictation, Helen was directed to look up "world" in the dictionary and specifically told not to use the most common definition, "planet Earth." The intended meaning was "natural grand division," a framework of perception. The "world of physics" and the "world of psychology" are two different worlds on the same planet. In the same way, the Course's "two worlds," the ego's world and the real world, are two ways of interpreting one creation, not two different places. When the Course says "there is no world," it means the ego's fearful interpretation of reality does not truly exist. It does not mean there is no planet. See Chapter 8 for full discussion.

12. Create vs. Make

The Course's original dictation distinguishes these two words carefully. God creates, from fullness, extending what is. The ego makes, from lack, reshaping what already exists. When the Course says "you made the world," it means the ego remade its perception of God's creation. When the Course speaks of "your creations," it means the soul you are building in partnership with the Adjuster, acts of true creation that share the nature of their divine source. See Chapter 6 for full discussion.
