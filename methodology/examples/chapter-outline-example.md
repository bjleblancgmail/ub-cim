# Chapter Outline Example: Finding God

**Source:** Real chapter outlines from "Finding God: A Companion to the Inward Journey" by Gabriel Rymberg

*This example shows what a completed Part 3 (Outline) output looks like for individual chapters.*

---

## The Chapter Outline Structure

Each chapter follows this framework:

| Element | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **Hook** | Story or anecdote that pulls the reader in |
| **Thesis** | The main point of this chapter |
| **Supporting Content** | Key ideas, concepts, arguments |
| **Stories** | Personal experiences that illustrate the points |
| **Reader's Key Takeaway** | What the reader should remember |
| **Callback/Segue** | Transition to the next chapter |

---

## Example: Chapter 1 - "Is This It?"

### Hook

**Story:** Israel, 2006, 2nd Lebanon war. Missiles flying. Me sleeping in the safe room in the basement, trying to listen to a comforting sound on the radio, and there is no reception. Will I be alive tomorrow morning?

### Thesis

The main reason we feel unfulfilled in our lives is because we are disconnected from God and our life's purpose.

### Supporting Content

- Something is not working in my life, something is missing, and no matter what I do, I cannot fill this "hole"
- If I'm honest with myself, I feel like shit most of the time
- Since most of my needs (physical, emotional, relationship, professional) are met, I am open to the possibility that the way I feel may be unrelated to these needs
- Becoming aware of this feeling and admitting it to myself is a significant accomplishment
- Once I become aware of this feeling, I can't continue with my life - a gnawing feeling doesn't let me be in peace
- The questions: "is this it?", "is this what life is about?", "I can die tonight - did I live my life to the fullest?" are going around in my mind
- I feel ashamed, as if something is wrong with me ("am I going crazy?")
- I don't know anyone around me I can talk to or share openly how I feel. I feel alone ("why isn't anyone talking about this?")
- I have never had a need for God in my life. I don't know what my life purpose is. I just know I feel like shit and want that to stop.

### Stories

Sitting in Yehudit Oliker's office, telling her that I quit and writing on a piece of paper "Why am I here?" She says, "one can get crazy by asking these questions."

### Reader's Key Takeaway

It is perfectly normal to feel this way. In fact, it is a blessing in disguise. And while it is far from pleasant, this is really the beginning of a wonderful journey to the "least explored destination in the universe". YOU.

### Callback/Segue to Next Chapter

It's OK. This is a good starting point. I am not alone. No, I can't do this alone. And I don't have to.

## Example: Chapter 2 - "I Need Help!"

### Hook

Sharing with a colleague my pain and frustration, and getting the recommendation to talk to Esti.

### Thesis

I try to "fix" whatever makes me unfulfilled. I realize that I won't make much progress unless I receive help from someone who has been in this place and found a way forward.

### Supporting Content

- There is only so much progress we can make alone in this path
- We are always surrounded by many agencies (human, other) who can help and are ready to do so, and are waiting for us to ask
- Asking for help is a necessary step in this life:
  - Not much progress can be done alone - this path requires us to be brutally honest about ourselves
  - Help will not be accepted by those who did not ask for it
  - The law of the universe is: Ask and you shall receive; seek and you shall find
- Who to ask for help from is also very important. It has to be the "right" kind of help from the "right" kind of person:
  - Someone that really has your best interest in mind ("pure motive")
  - Someone with the wisdom to see you and impart the advice you need at the right moment

### Life Tool

Follow the "Breadcrumbs of Synchronicity"

### Stories

Listening to my Yoga teacher talk about the Yoga Sutras of Patanjali. Going to the library and getting a very bare bones copy. Reading it in one sitting and thinking "I want to live like this. I want to be with people who practice this."

### Reader's Key Takeaway

Even though your personal journey inward is unique, we all face very similar challenges. This journey may prove to be the most difficult of all. Shouldn't you use all the help you can get?

### Callback/Segue to Next Chapter

Breadcrumbs of synchronicity as a lesson for life. One thing leads to another. The sum total of what we've experienced so far prepares us for the next phase.

## Example: Chapter 3 - "Did I Really Create This Life?"

### Hook

What is meant by "we create our own reality"? Who is this mysterious "I" that creates his own reality?

### Thesis

I realize I create my own reality and learn that my external reality is a reflection of my inner reality. I learn to accept total responsibility for my life. I decide to set out on a journey inward, because that is where I will find what I'm looking for - Me.

### Supporting Content

- A healthy dose of self-reflection and honesty with which to ask and answer: "what is not working in my life?"
- While we examine the reality outside, in effect we are looking for clues to our inner reality - "what is my life situation teaching me about me?"
- Some of things we may want:
  - The end of suffering
  - To be happy
  - To be fulfilled
  - More of...
- **Life Tool:** Our external reality is always a reflection of our inner reality
- Before we can hope for change, we must accept complete responsibility. Our choices, conscious or not, determine our life
- Why do we choose what we do? Can we choose differently?
- If we desire external change... we must turn inward, even if we have no idea what that may mean or require
- To take the first step, what we can do... is ask for a guide

### Stories

Something about Ho'oponopono and absolute responsibility (Dr. Hew Len's interviews and book)

### Reader's Key Takeaway

Anytime I take responsibility I can build my response-ability, and can choose to create differently.

### Callback/Segue to Next Chapter

The first thing I choose to do is to ask for help and accept it when it comes.

## Why This Outline Format Works

### 1. The Outline is Your GPS

During the writing phase, you don't have to think "what am I writing today?" The outline tells you:
- What chapter you're on
- What the hook/opening story is
- What point you're making
- What stories to include
- How to end and transition

You just open the outline and write.

### 2. Each Element Serves a Purpose

- **Hook** = Pull the reader in (emotional engagement)
- **Thesis** = Give them the main idea (clarity)
- **Supporting Content** = Build the argument (substance)
- **Stories** = Make it real (connection)
- **Key Takeaway** = Make it memorable (retention)
- **Callback** = Move them forward (momentum)

### 3. Placeholders Are OK

Notice chapters 7-11 in the original outline had `<bla>` placeholders. That's fine at this stage. The structure is there; you'll fill in the content as you develop each chapter.

### 4. Level of Detail Varies

Chapter 1 has very detailed supporting content. Later chapters are sparser. You develop each chapter more fully as you approach writing it.

---

## Your Task in Part 3

Create an outline like this for every chapter of your book. When you sit down to write, you should be able to open your outline and immediately know:

1. What story opens this chapter
2. What point you're making
3. What supporting ideas to cover
4. What personal stories to include
5. What you want the reader to remember
6. How to transition to the next chapter

This removes all decision fatigue from the writing process.
